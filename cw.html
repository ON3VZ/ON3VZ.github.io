My CW Journey

Notes, experiences and milestones while learning Morse code.

Step 01 · Start
Why CW?
CW is the most efficient mode for DX contacts with low power. With just a few watts you can reach the world. The challenge is what excites me.
Step 02 · Method
Koch Method
I use the Koch method: starting with 2 characters at full speed (20 WPM) and only expanding when recognition is above 90%. Farnsworth timing helps a lot.
Step 03 · Tools
LCWO & G4FON
LCWO.net for daily online practice. G4FON Koch Trainer for offline sessions. The goal is at least 15 minutes every day.
In progress
Memorising Q-codes
The Q-codes on this page are my first memory aid. By listening to them repeatedly I want to learn to recognise them automatically on the band.
Tip
Listening on the band
Listening on 40m CW (7.000–7.040 MHz) every day to hear real QSOs. Start by recognising callsigns and RST reports.
Goal
First CW QSO
The ultimate goal: completing a full CW QSO — exchanging callsign, report, name and QTH. Every day one step closer to this milestone.
