Chapter II

HF

Two antennas. Two jobs. A dedicated low-band horizontal for 80m and 40m, and a full-size vertical for 20m through 10m DX. Class C licence active on 10m, 15m and 20m.

● Active
Transceiver · HF / 6m

ICOM IC-7300 MK II

The heart of the HF shack. The IC-7300 MK II is a direct-sampling SDR HF transceiver covering 160m through 6m (including the 4m band in the European version). Compared to the original IC-7300 it adds an HDMI output for an external monitor, a LAN port for PC-less remote control, a second USB-C port with dual COM + audio, dedicated RX antenna inputs (SMA), a built-in CW decoder, and lower power consumption. The real-time spectrum scope and waterfall make band-hunting intuitive. The definitive compact HF station.

IronWave 6
A true DX-oriented vertical for the upper HF bands — 10m, 12m, 15m, 17m, 20m, and usually 30m as well. The full-size radiator and a ground system of 32 buried radials give it a genuinely low RF ground loss, translating to low-angle vertical radiation and excellent DX performance. No tuner needed on the target bands.

32 buried radials form a high-quality RF ground plane — the foundation of any good ground-mounted vertical. This is what separates a "DX vertical" from a compromise stick.
