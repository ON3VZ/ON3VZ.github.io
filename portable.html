Open-Wire Doublet
9kW · 600Ω Ladder Line
A full-size HF doublet fed with 600Ω open-wire ladder line — the most efficient feeder available for a portable multiband antenna. Unlike coaxial cable, open-wire line has extremely low loss even at high SWR, which means the IC-7300's built-in ATU can tune it across virtually the entire HF spectrum with minimal loss.

Why ladder line beats coax here: Coaxial cable becomes very lossy when operated at high SWR — a multiband doublet will have high SWR on most bands. Open-wire line (600Ω) has such low conductor loss that even at 10:1 SWR, the loss remains negligible. At low power (10W), every fraction of a dB matters — ladder line gets the signal out where coax would absorb it.

11m Citizen Band · 27 MHz

No licence needed. A local POTA-inspired family radio network for the Ardennes holiday house.

On family holidays in the Ardennes, we built a local CB radio network using 27 MHz (Citizen Band). No amateur radio licence required — fully legal in Belgium with CE-marked equipment. The full family can participate from day one — a great way to get the kids involved in radio too.

The setup combines the two PNI HP 82 handhelds (4W FM) with a proper base station (Albrecht AE 5900 Mini SSB) and a mobile car station — giving 12W SSB range of 20–60 km vs the 2–8 km of the handhelds. The holiday house becomes the radio hub.
